In my concentration of historic preservation and restoration, I've had to complete four courses, each with their own projects. The first class included a group project with an adaptive re-use of the Lafayette Building and next door church in South Bend, IN into a boutique hotel. Sceondly, the historic construction class had two independent research and illustration projects about historic building techniques, along with hands on demonstrations of woodworking and preservation techniques. My third class was about the research and documentation of buildings, and in a group we did measured drawings of the Comfort Station and Navarre Cabin in South Bend, IN. The final class taught us about the history of american architecture and allowed us to research a certain architectural subtopic of our choosing.
